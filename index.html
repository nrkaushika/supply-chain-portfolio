EWM concepts demonstrated

Every tool is grounded in real SAP Extended Warehouse Management (EWM) logic — not just terminology.

Storage Type Search Sequence
EWM evaluates storage types in priority order at putaway. LG01 (Zone A) checked first — first match wins. Configured in SPRO. Each warehouse product group gets its own sequence.
Warehouse Task (WT)
EWM's internal movement instruction — source bin to destination bin, with material, quantity, batch, and handling unit. Grouped into Warehouse Orders (WO). Confirmed via RF scanner. Transaction: /SCWM/TORD · /SCWM/TO_CONF
Quality Management (QM) Hold
Damaged stock bypasses normal putaway entirely — routed to LG09 (Quality Inspection storage type), segregated from unrestricted stock. QA32 usage decision required before release or return to vendor.
FEFO — First Expiry First Out
Near-expiry stock routed to LG08 (Near-Expiry Zone) and always picked before standard stock regardless of receipt date. Mandatory in pharma regulated environments. Shelf life threshold: 90 days.
Batch Traceability
Batch number tracked from GR posting through Warehouse Task creation, bin determination, and confirmation. Required for pharma compliance and product recall readiness. Transaction: MB56 (Batch Where-Used List).
Supply Chain KPIs — OTIF & Fill Rate
On Time In Full (OTIF) and order fill rate are the primary KPIs on every supply chain analyst dashboard. Tracked in SAP via delivery reliability reports in EWM and SD. Target: ≥ 95% for pharma distributors.
